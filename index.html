Dhokla

Dhokla is a savory, spongy cake originating from Gujarat, India. Made from fermented rice and chickpea flour batter, it's seasoned with spices and steamed. Typically served as a snack or breakfast, it's known for its tangy flavor and light texture, often accompanied by chutneys.

Idli

Idli is a popular South Indian dish made from fermented rice and black lentil batter. It's steamed to create soft, fluffy, and slightly tangy cakes. Idli is a staple breakfast item and is often served with chutneys or sambar, a flavorful lentil-based stew. It's renowned for its lightness, simplicity, and nutritious qualities.

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy pancake from South India made with fermented rice and lentil batter. It's cooked until golden brown and served with sambar and chutney. Known for its versatility and savory taste, dosa is a beloved dish enjoyed across India and beyond.

Chole Bhatura

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish featuring spicy chickpeas (chole) in a flavorful gravy, served with deep-fried bread (bhature). It's a popular and indulgent street food, enjoyed for its rich flavors and satisfying combination of textures.

Dal Makhni

Dal Makhani is a creamy and flavorful Indian lentil dish made with black lentils, kidney beans, butter, and cream, simmered to perfection with aromatic spices. It's a rich and indulgent vegetarian staple in Indian cuisine, loved for its velvety texture and savory taste.
